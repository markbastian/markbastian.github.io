Clojure on the Rise

May 28, 2017

Introduction
It's been a while since I've maintained my blog over at fn-code. Rather than belabor the details, here's a summary of why I haven't been actively blogging for over a year:
  • I changed jobs and moved from New Mexico to Idaho at the end of 2015 which entailed a lot of disruption in my life.
  • I have other interests and have to balance my time. Family, personal coding projects, learning IoT, reading books, playing games, etc., etc., etc.
  • Google modified Drive to no longer serve up JavaScript libraries. Finding and implementing a good workaround has been a time suck.
  • Blogging is hard. If you don't believe me, try to write an informative and interesting post. Then continue to do so on a regular basis.

I'm back - I hope
The above said, I keep coming back to my blog and thinking I really need to get back into it. The recent kerfuffle on Twitter regarding the supposed death of Clojure has pushed me over the edge and I am going to try to get regular posts out again, starting with this one. Clojure is alive and well in my life - and it explains my current silence
When I started at Clearwater Analytics in 2015 I was basically the only Clojure developer at a Java-centric company. We had two other fans that I knew about and a large number of people were familiar with Rich Hickey's classics (esp. "The Value of Values"), but nobody was writing Clojure professionally. Cl I have become the de facto Chief Clojure Evangelist at my company.
